Introduction



Hackathon is an event conducted with the idea to bring people from different walks of life, possessing different sets of skills, to work together and solve some real-world problems using their attained skills. The most important part of any hackathon is team building and utilization of the resources available may it be in the form of knowledge, expertise, or material. Hackathon will make the teams work at their full potential. Hackathon provides an environment where all teams work under time and resource constraints. This helps bring out the best results.

Flow of Event


1. The event will commence on 6/04/2022, at 6.00 Pm, with the formation of teams and allotment of chosen problem statements, which is to be conducted online.
2. The team will be given ample time of 2 days to build the software and hardware.
3. All the teams are required to gather at the specified labs to work on the problem and mentor walkthrough.
4. The participants will be free to work at the specified labs in the time given.
5. The teams are allowed to work in the labs on 8/04/2022 too.
6. On 8/04/2022, the teams will be called for the presentation of their solution to
the problem statements. All teams must gather whenever asked to.

Note: Participants are required to bring their own laptops and other devices (if required).

Who can Participate?


Students from all branches of engineering can participate in the event. The event is open for all engineering undergraduates, irrespective of college or university. Students with the knowledge of computer coding and problem-solving skills can participate.

Rules and Regulations

1. Registering for the hackathon is entirely free of charge. 2. Each team must be given a name as chosen by its members. The team’s name must be neutral in nature and must not be too flashy. 3. Participating teams are required to provide correct information about each and every team member during the online application. Any ambiguous entry will not be considered. 4. Eligible Submissions will be evaluated by a panel of judges selected by the Administrator (the “Judges”). Judging may take place in one or more rounds with one or more panels of Judges, at the discretion of the Administrator. 5. Judges are not required to test the Application and may choose to judge based solely on the text description, images, and video provided in the Submission. 6. Administrators reserve the right in their sole discretion to disqualify any individual or Maker it finds to be actually or presenting the appearance of tampering with the entry process or the operation of the Hackathon or to be acting in violation of these Official Rules or in a manner that is inappropriate, not in the best interests of this Hackathon, or a violation of any applicable law or regulation. 7. You will be bound by and comply with these Official Rules and the decisions of the Administrator, and/or the Hackathon Judges which are binding and final in all matters relating to the Hackathon. 8. Any kind of cheating and plagiarism will lead to disqualification of the team and it will be barred to participate in further events. 9. Participating teams are required to coordinate with the management team of IIC for the smooth execution of the event. 10. Students are required to bring their own laptops and other devices (if required).
