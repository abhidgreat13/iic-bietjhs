Introduction

We have been using bridges for centuries to cross the obstacles such as valleys, rough terrain or bodies of water. To make the bridge last longer, it has to be made stronger and more durable. In this event we are going to conduct a competition of constructing a truss bridge using popsicle sticks of standard dimensions and the strength of the bridges will be examined by applying load to the Centre of the top side of the bridge.


Flow of Event


Introduction:
The participants will be briefed about the details of the event. All the rules and regulations will be stated again for the flawless conduction of the event.

Team Building:
Students will then be allowed to make a team consisting of 4-5 members. .

Working Time:
The working time can be flexible, depending upon the complexity of the Structure designing.

Presentation:
After the completion of working time, the teams will be called for the final presentation of their stable structure.

Testing:
The final submitted structure will be evaluated based on its design and also using weights.

Award Distribution:
The team whose structure withstands the maximum pressure and does not collapse under simulated conditions will be considered winner. The winning team will be awarded certificates, medals and sponsor rewards.
